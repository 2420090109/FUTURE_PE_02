# FUTURE_PE_02 — AI Content Marketing using UGC Ads

**Track:** Prompt Engineering (PE)
**Task:** Task 2 — AI Content Marketing using UGC Ads
**Tools Used:** Claude (claude.ai)
**Product Chosen:** Closeup Toothpaste — a real, widely available consumer brand

---

## 1. Objective

Generate a complete UGC-style ad content pack (scripts, hooks, CTAs, captions) for a real product using structured prompts, optimized for conversion rather than generic brand content.

---

## 2. Prompt Engineering Approach

Closeup is a well-known brand built around freshness and confidence in social/personal moments. Rather than copying existing brand ads, each prompt below was structured to generate **original UGC-style content** — first-person, casual, platform-native — built around that same confidence/freshness angle, but in an authentic "real person" voice rather than polished ad copy.

Each prompt enforced:
- A specific **hook angle** (relatability, curiosity, before/after, myth-busting, urgency)
- **Platform-native tone** (Instagram/TikTok casual, not scripted)
- A **problem → solution → CTA** arc
- Explicit instruction to avoid sounding like a traditional advertisement

---

## 3. Prompts & Outputs

### 🔹 3.1 Multiple Hooks (Scroll-Stopping Openers)

**Prompt used:**
> "Write 5 different scroll-stopping opening hooks (1 sentence each) for a UGC-style video ad about Closeup toothpaste, a brand known for freshness and confidence. Each hook should use a different angle: relatability, curiosity, before/after, myth-busting, and urgency. Keep them casual, like a real person talking to camera, not a brand."

**Output:**

> 1. **(Relatability)** "Okay but why did no one tell me fresh breath could actually change your confidence this much?"
> 2. **(Curiosity)** "I didn't expect a toothpaste to be the reason I stopped avoiding close conversations."
> 3. **(Before/After)** "Me before switching my toothpaste vs. me now walking into every conversation confident."
> 4. **(Myth-busting)** "I used to think all toothpaste basically did the same thing. I was wrong."
> 5. **(Urgency)** "If you've got a date, interview, or big day coming up, do this one thing first."

---

### 🔹 3.2 Full UGC Ad Script (Problem–Solution–CTA Format)

**Prompt used:**
> "Using hook #1 above, write a full 30-second UGC ad script for Closeup toothpaste in a problem-solution-CTA structure. Include short scene directions in brackets. Tone: authentic, first-person, like a genuine recommendation from a friend, not a scripted ad."

**Output:**

> **[Scene: talking head, natural lighting, casual setting]**
> "Okay but why did no one tell me fresh breath could actually change your confidence this much?
>
> **[Scene: close-up, smiling]**
> "I used to hesitate before getting close in conversations — like, physically hold back a little. Small thing, but it added up.
>
> **[Scene: holding the product]**
> "Then I actually started using Closeup properly, twice a day, no skipping. Not exaggerating, but I noticed the difference within days.
>
> **[Scene: back to camera, relaxed and confident]**
> "Now I don't think twice before leaning in for a conversation, a laugh, whatever. If something this small can do that, it's worth doing right. Grab a tube and try it for a week — you'll notice it too."

### 🔹 3.4 Additional Hook Variant (Local/Relationship Angle)

**Prompt used:**
> "Write one more hook and CTA specifically playing on Closeup's classic brand theme of 'closeness' in relationships — first date or close friendships — keeping it UGC-style and not overly cheesy."

**Output:**

> **Hook:** "Nobody tells you the real prep for a first date isn't the outfit — it's this."
> **CTA:** "Small habit, big difference. Try Closeup before your next big moment — you'll feel it."

---

## 4. Key Learnings

- Naming a **specific hook angle** (relatability vs. urgency vs. myth-busting) in the prompt produced sharper, more distinct outputs than asking for "hooks" generically.
- For an established brand like Closeup, instructing the AI to build on the brand's existing emotional territory (confidence, closeness) — without copying real ad scripts — kept the content on-brand while staying original.
- Explicitly telling the AI to avoid "sounding like an ad" was necessary; without it, outputs drifted toward polished brand copy instead of authentic UGC tone.
- Reusing one hook across the full script → caption pipeline kept messaging consistent across formats, which mirrors how real UGC campaigns are built.
